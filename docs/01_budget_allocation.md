# 01 — Budget Allocation

**Question this section answers:** *How much money does UCLA Men's Basketball actually have to spend on its 2026-27 roster, and how did we get to that number?*

## The top of the funnel: UCLA's athletic revenue

UCLA Athletics runs a meaningful operating budget funded primarily by media rights (Big Ten), donor contributions, ticket sales, and NCAA/conference distributions. The move from the Pac-12 to the Big Ten — effective 2024-25 — was the single most important financial event in the department's recent history:

- **Big Ten media rights (FY25):** $61.2M — more than triple the $19.9M UCLA received in its final Pac-12 year (Yahoo Sports, Feb 2026).
- **CFP / bowl / NCAA tournament distributions:** ~$13M (up from $8.7M in the Pac-12).
- **Accumulated athletic department debt:** $167.7M as of recent reporting, a meaningful constraint on discretionary spending.

That last bullet matters: unlike Kentucky or Arkansas — schools paying from a position of surplus — UCLA is funding rev-share while simultaneously paying down debt. It constrains the ceiling and probably makes the department less willing to go far over the $20.5M cap with supplemental donor-funded NIL.

## The cap: $20.5M for 2025-26, ~$21.3M for 2026-27

Under the House v. NCAA settlement (final approval June 2025), Division I schools may share up to **$20.5M** with athletes for the 2025-26 academic year. The cap grows ~4% annually, putting the **2026-27 cap at roughly $21.3M** (projected to hit ~$32.9M by 2034-35).

UCLA AD Martin Jarmond publicly committed to funding the full cap (LA Times, June 2025) — "sharing the highest allowable amount." This is the baseline: **UCLA's 2026-27 revenue-share pool is $21.3M.**

## The split: House back-damages formula

UCLA has indicated it will use **Judge Wilken's back-damages formula** as its allocation model — the same formula used to distribute the $2.8B in back-pay from the settlement. That formula:

| Sport | Back-damages share | UCLA 2026-27 allocation (est.) |
|---|---|---|
| Football | 75% | **$15.97M** |
| Men's basketball | 15% | **$3.19M** |
| Women's basketball | 5% | **$1.06M** |
| All other sports | 5% | **$1.06M** |
| **Total** | **100%** | **$21.28M** |

*Source: LA Times interview with Jarmond (June 7, 2025); confirmed pattern across most Power Four schools per Ross Dellenger (Yahoo Sports).*

**This is the core assumption of this project:** UCLA MBB's 2026-27 rev-share allocation is approximately **$3.19M**.

Reasonable people might push this up or down:
- **Up:** If UCLA chose to weight MBB more heavily (Kentucky reportedly allocated ~25-30% to MBB; that'd give UCLA ~$5.3-6.4M). UCLA's football-first conference identity argues against this, but a coaching change or a particularly soft football year could shift it.
- **Down:** Title IX legal risk (not yet tested) could eventually require more women's sport allocation, squeezing MBB. Most practitioners expect this to remain unlitigated in 2026-27.

## The off-cap layer: third-party NIL

Rev-share is not the whole story. Third-party NIL deals — collective money, brand endorsements, personal appearances — still exist outside the cap, though they must be reported to the NIL Go clearinghouse if over $600. Based on market data:

- **Power Four MBB total roster cost average:** $7M–$10M (Opendorse, via On3).
- **Average rev-share allocation to MBB:** $4.2M (Opendorse).
- **Implied NIL layer:** ~$3–6M per Power Four MBB roster on top of rev-share.

For UCLA specifically, the LA market premium should, in theory, boost third-party NIL (larger media market, adjacent to Hollywood, historical brand strength). In practice, the Bruin Athletic Fund and affiliated collective (Champions of Westwood / similar) have not been reported at Kentucky or BYU levels of donor generosity. A reasonable mid-point assumption: **$2–4M in third-party NIL** available to MBB.

## UCLA MBB 2026-27 total roster budget

| Source | Low | Midpoint | High |
|---|---|---|---|
| Rev-share allocation (15% of $21.3M cap) | $3.19M | $3.19M | $3.19M |
| Third-party NIL (collective + brand) | $2.0M | $3.0M | $4.5M |
| **Total roster budget** | **$5.2M** | **$6.2M** | **$7.7M** |

**Base case for this project: $6.2M total MBB roster budget for 2026-27.**

That's the number every downstream section of this project is built around. It's below the Power Four average ($7-10M) and well below blue-blood elite spending ($10M+), which is consistent with UCLA's debt constraints and football-first allocation.

## Context: where does this put UCLA in the market?

Reported 2025-26 MBB roster costs for reference (per The Athletic coaches survey, CBS Sports, nil-ncaa.com):

UCLA's $6.2M base case puts it roughly at the **Power Four median** — a defensible, realistic number. Not Kentucky money. Not Iowa money either.

## What this budget implies downstream

At $6.2M across 15 roster spots, the math forces tradeoffs:

- **Average per player:** ~$413K (assuming equal allocation — nobody does this).
- **Star + supporting cast:** One $2M player leaves $4.2M for 14 spots = $300K average for the rest.
- **Balanced veteran:** Three $1M+ players leaves $3.2M for 12 spots = $267K average.
- **Flywheel:** No player over $800K, average of $413K.
